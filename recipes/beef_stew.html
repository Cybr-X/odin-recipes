Beef Stew Recipe

Description

Beef stew is a comforting and hearty dish, perfect for cold days. It features tender chunks of beef simmered slowly with vegetables like carrots, potatoes, and onions in a rich, flavorful broth. The slow cooking process allows the flavors to meld together, creating a delicious and satisfying meal that's both nutritious and filling.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of beef chuck, cut into chunks
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 4 cups of beef broth
  • 1 cup of red wine (optional)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 potatoes, diced

Steps

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add the beef chunks and brown them on all sides. Remove the beef and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, saut√© the chopped onion, minced garlic, and diced carrots until they are softened.
  3. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for about a minute.
  4. Return the browned beef to the pot. Add the beef broth, red wine (if using), bay leaves, thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  5. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for about 2 hours, or until the beef is tender.
  6. Add the diced potatoes to the pot and continue to simmer for another 30 minutes, or until the potatoes are cooked through.
  7. Remove the bay leaves before serving. Enjoy your delicious beef stew!
